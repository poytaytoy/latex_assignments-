    $$||x - x_n|| < \epsilon/2 + \epsilon/2 = \epsilon$$

    Let $\epsilon > 0$ be given. Select a $N$ where $\forall n,m \geq N$, $D(A_n, A_m) < \epsilon/2$.  For all $n \geq N$, let $x_n$ be where $d(x_n , A) = d(A_n, A)$ since it is a maxmimum. From class, $\frac{1}{2^{k}}$ converges sufficiently that $(y_k)$ is Cauchy. $\mathbb{R}^2$ is complete, so $y_k \rightarrow x \in \mathbb{R}^2$. From our proof of (a), $x \in A$. We then consider that: 
    \begin{align*}
